A skull here, a jawbone there. A piece of green spine, porous algae brushed dot in the exclamation mark of the mountain’s Get A Backbone. The ribcage broken out from, a monument to who-knows-what’s freedom. A clustering of leg bones, as if seed scattered by a great hand upon the pony-haunted steepness. It could be one sheep all this. The hidden sun the ramming home of his penis in the post-death act that birthed her who birthed him. Wind in your right temple like the wrong hand. Icy grip that has you grin in the vicinity of its lethality. Knowing you will not overexpose the imaginative photon graph of yourself. Quantum scribblings.

Down there is The Vision. I had assumed it was a good one, but the unbeautiful cluster of corrugated roofs suggest it could be a vision of hell. They looked red to her, the black mountains. Yes, I said, but it is red on black. The wind was of Thee Wylds. The vegetation was a treacherous patchwork of clues as to the wearer of such a strangely lovely coat. Growing fur coat unseperate from what must be a Black Mountain skin. Is England an opening in her dress, a gentle, sensual invitation to a seeing of her softer face — or is she someone else? I feel her move from one place, one state, to another. Sense the shrugging on and off of what for most is moveless.

The unbetween of bracken and bilberry. So clear the change, accordment with what we call altitude, but is a cluster of feelings, a cluster of feelings which is a sense in itself, not transcoded into a certain, or somewhat certain, number of categories. Cold is cold not a kind of touch. The wind is not a skin. What is the wet? Clear vision, musical tickling of the grasses of your inner ear? Your inside outer here? It is clear the change, clear to the mind, and just as clear is its jitter and jag, the fuzzy unproximations of the two world’s unbetween. It is a line.

And they are lines that the sheep walk. They cannot make these contours, but they wouldn’t be there were they not brought on out. Nature has no outline, but this is the supernatural itself. The lines are clear enough to me. Who perceives they have been taught to perceive, and then taught to realize that I see what I cannot see, that what is is the being that cannot be, but who I am we

our reality, which is not this, nothing we make of it, rather the imagination of what is really there, which must trans… oon… transoon… to… who is really here.
