Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010223

 

24th February

08:14

The previous entry is something I needed to say to myself. The rest I’m not sure. I’m quite tired at the moment, from some hard work yesterday but also just anyway. I had a very revitalising and solid little meditation yesterday. Very clear. I sat and it was just there. I drifted a few times but without really losing touch and came back to it easily. It felt a little like riding a motionless horse, as if I had to still myself in order to mount it, a kind of moving backwards in a sense, and then voila, just sat there. The Surungama Sutra is vivid, rich, and clear. It requires a clear mind and sharp attention to follow the arguments and make sure you’re catching the examples — sometimes feels like there’s something going on with the examples beyond the argument, and the visionary passages are really brilliant: you really feel gathered, one of the infinite worlds drawn close to this lovely hall and beautiful garden to hear the teaching.

But the bunny stuff I’m not sure. I feel like I wanted to tell some other story in which the focus was just those first nights he ran away, before he first broke his leg — to imagine a tale therein, of his meeting the Hare people and encountering their strange religion… but that has got me going back over my story of, with, and around him.

So I thought perhaps I could just do that, tell that tale, but for one thing it seems too much — too much for the next month I mean — and for another thing it feels like perhaps I have to write the other chapters first, and so maybe all this work is meant for another time, another place… upstairs in my workshop, piecing it together with a little more distance?

I guess I’m not finished writing it. All these are meat and bones in the still becoming broth of it. I'm still living it.

I thought I could do the work retracing the bunny’s steps, and then whenever I got to a place where he went adventuring I could let my imagination leap off after him. That’s not a bad idea but I’m just not sure it is for now.

Just keep up my diary maybe.
