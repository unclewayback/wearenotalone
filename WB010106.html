Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010106

 

January 25th, 2023

07:17, WB

It is Wednesday, says the little scrap of a dustbin schedule we use instead of a calender this year. I’m better with the date than the day. I think I will make my own calender next year — start an annual (durr) Winding Brook calender. Or calenderender. Awe calenderenderror. Eek.

Had one of those moments yesterdodd when I saw myself as irrevocably oblique. Not, actually. This just an overreaction to delving into old materials and finding them even less comprehensible than I thought they might be at the time. It is not everything I do, just sometimes seems a larger percentage than expected.

Broadly speaking communication difficulties. Tempting even to split it into different people, all using the same set of mouth-pulling unhandles and blah-blah tokens. Mister Pass-the-butter is nice and direct and makes a good dinner party guest, at least a somewhat reliable one. Nothing wrong with him hey fellow?

But who is that wordy worm-tongue bird-heart trying to say what? It is one thing to write it down and unrecite it silently in the privacy of ones own unliving doom, quite another to go splashing it all about in the communal swimming pool of our day-to-day business. Metaphoreally mixed-up fool trying to use the lilo of our lingo to tilt its windmill towards our speedboat!

No, the idea is more… that there is something or someone in me who does somehow need to quite deeply fuck with the language it has been given in order to express itself. The issue is whether or not that is actually someone with something to say, or if it isn’t simply the emotional bottom burps of Mister Pass-the-butter.

It does seem like language itself is leading me astray sometimes too. None of this was really what I wanted to say, and now I feel whatever that was getting further away. I know that it wasn’t easy to say somehow, emotionally, and that is part of what I wonder: if the somersaulting wordage isn’t just the unintentional warpage of a psychic knot?

And if so, would that be one of those knots folk would consider it unlucky to untie?

Whatever the case may be, I feel I can say this: that somehow I have not been given, or not been able to find, in what is commonly available, something that some part of me feels it needs in order to express itself.

Likely this is part of the feeling of poetry or art, but it is not separate from what I could consider my run-of-the-mill communication problems. Or, seeing as how most would find me a good communicator in normal conversation, then what appears a communication problem to me is actually some kind of emotional issue, and one that is deeply wound up with whatever my, at least, creativity might be.

It appears a communication problem to me because it is mostly myself I am struggling to make sense to, although not of — I mean I am trying to make sense of more than just myself, it is just that… how do you make sense of anything else if you cannot make sense of yourself? But then, if the self itself is non-existent nonsense…

 of this unmagined tongue

13:34

I have been thinking about making a collage of my notebooks and photographs in the form of an eScroll. It could start with a perforation mark and a comment to the effect that the entirety of the history of everything prior to this point is metaphorical Bog Roll. Toilet Paper. And the entire history of the human race, or Cosmic Ethnicity or whatever it is, is akin to this perforated line. This line that is just an easy-rip point between the everything before and the potential nothing after. The point being that, even if our story is taken to reach 200,000 years back (forgetting the Ultraterrestrial Hypothesis for the moment, as well as the impossibility of really separating cause from effect, at all) it is still just a perforation between everything and nothing, and the most we can hope for, which is still really rather a lot, is that we might one day be part of something that amounts to a single-sheet of two-ply toilet paper, which is itself probably just one of many rolls in the almost forgotten cupboard of a rather foresightful, or at least insightful, Superrorentity who somehow manages to feel not that much more significant than you, as not even the writer of this nonsensical nothinghood, would, if you were me imagining that you might never exist, or something.

(Oh yes, that was it: Origami Hospital. That was the name of the hospital I hope to go to if I ever have to go back in to one. I only thought of it because of the scroll/bogroll train of thought: I was thinking, if we do manage to make our story as significant as a sheet of sheet paper, rather than just hanging around at the perforation here, forever, then I will give us extra points for folding ourselves up into some kind of Hospitable Origami as a sign of respect for the next divine arsehole who happens to occupy this Universal Room.)

17:48

The bathroom has been retitled The Throne Room. It is that, for me, the toilet (in particular our little écopot) : The Throne of All Good Thin Kings. I do enjoy a good shit thought, as you know, and have not gotten too fat off the back of my people. It really is a peaceful place now, too. Clean and bright, white and light green, chrome and tile and wood and glass. Plants.

I’ve just been fitting the toilet today, you see, mounting the wooden box over the old concrete footing, which we will keep in place for the time being. I made a bespoke wooden toilet roll holder as well, which holds up to 8 rolls. I put my beer on top and sat on my throne and surveyed my realm. It is a gentle, happy country, none too shy of giggle or parp.

Just missing a few final touches, like the Winding Brook notice board — a cork board next to the loo that I want to pin our first map to, alongside the obligatory funny bunny postcards. It is my office, too, you see. Office, Throne Room, Great Hall: the secret spiritual heart of Winding Brook.

I do believe I am only half joking.

18:22

I think the field formerly known as The Ruin Field, which wasn’t really a name, is now called Stargazey. It just struck me, on my way to get a bit more wood for the fire, how open it feels since the lightning struck the hawthorn down, and that word popped into my head looking at it, just like that: Stargazey. It is roughly south-facing from the house, and indeed is the way I most look at the stars — get a big show of Orion there all winter, and have Mars, Jupiter, and Venus all visible just after dark at the moment, arcing off towards the west. Well, it is cloudy tonight, but I guess they’re there just the same. Just the same old diff’rent from yesterday, like ev’rythink.

Stargazey. The place where Fae Lurk and Amulet’s shall sit. One of our most precious and variable, and lived in, fields. I shall have to carry Leviathan out there one of these nights.

Or one of these winters.
