Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010219

 

20th February

07:22

Heard Fox, early this morning, just a sentence or two of that slightly strangled tongue, strange and high-pitched. Enough to see a mad glistening grin in the dark of my mind, twinkling martian eyes peering through a theatrical fox mask.

It has been a long time. Maybe seven years since I last saw them here. There was the interlude with that vixen who pulled me in a fugue state to her roadside corpse. Her coat hung around a while, bounced off Little Odin’s presence to work some magick in my soul. But no living fox physically present.

Not since the one we used to see in the early morning light that summer, hunting in the long grass, delighting me with its great high pounce, something I’d never seen an urban fox do. I guess I’d never seen them hunt, only scavenge.

Gave me a poem.

The way you question the house,
 I have questioned the woods.
 I too have sniffed the air
 and stretched my neck to peer
 into the calling place with a gaze
 that became my body.

Your face! — between us
 a broken bridge over which
 something nonetheless
 comes to pass.

And now I’m smiling!
 I never saw you
 pounce like that into
 bin bag or kebab box!
 I never saw your tail
 so perfectly calligraphic of…

Not-Fox. You’ve been
 a different creature, painted
 orange sodium shadow
 of yourself, bent towards night dog,
 sad surprise deprived ghost.

Here you are more real, shockingly
 even more a spirit: you take reality
 and run with it, sly sight magnet,
 form enlivened talkative silence.
 Name no one else can say.

I find myself wondering if it isn’t time to try to write that little story, the one I thought could be called Our God Is Fox. Or something like that.

An adventure Mushishi the bunny has. Had.

To challenge myself to take this somewhere else a little while. Risk a failure, but guarantee myself a few gulps of fresh air. Find one of those places where I’m not, to lie down and disappear for a while. Become the beasts of an imaginary God. Or the reality of its imagination. A reality, another facet of its infinite truth.

Sounds good in theory. A bit overblown. Do you think you’ll manage it?

I don’t know. It’s happening though. Let me have a look at the calender. I’ll do it for Lent. I have a structure in mind, although I want to leave all the details in this muttering uncertain cloud I can feel uncrowding the edges of my world. Wait…

Lent starts on Wednesday the 22nd — Cendres — that’s got to be Ash Wednesday and that’s what that means right? Tomorrow must be Shrove Tuesday. St Félix. I asked Chou to make me pancakes yesterday. My cream runs out tomorrow. No coffee, no dairy, no meat, and maybe even no gluten. Go easy on Sundays if you must.

Wednesday to Sunday you can introduce… let Mushi introduce himself and set things up. Then you have four weeks which can correspond to four nights. His first little set of escapades, that will end with his first broken leg. Then you have five days to conclude it, before April Fool’s.

I’ll meditate every day and do a little routine for my body. I have too much belly at the moment and my back has been stiff. I’ll start work upstairs again, as well, just little by little screwing some plasterboard up.

From April Fool’s until Easter I will write a week of entries that can come at the start of this section of Diary, Winter 2022. I don’t yet know whether I will make it generally available but I will comb back through it and put it together as the ebook I was thinking, even if it ends up being just for me and you. Maybe I’ll do two, a raw unexpurgiated one, and a selections from.

After Easter, soon enough, I expect Cuckoo will announce the real meta-calendrical Fool’s Day, whereupon I will begin stage two of The Cuckoo Working, codenamed Imaginary Council.

I might restrict myself to my notebook for that, and only writing when Cuckoo calls. That should take us up to Solstice, thereabouts, and I’ll start another round of this then, I guess.

Breaking Convention will happen in the midst of this Cuckoo Working, near the start, I mean, and I might take some notes on that, regardless of cuckoo or not, because the book I’ll be working in has the same Labyrinth they chose for their logo on its cover. We’ll see.

So that’s the program. I’ll try to stick to it. Would be nice to have a simple website up by Beltane, and the first installment of the diary finished by then.

A wee while after hearing Fox this morning, Little Owl hooted hello in a half dream. Sat on one of the posts in the misty blue dawn, out front. I came around the corner and saw it there with its back to me, and then Chou followed. I motioned to her to be slow and quiet and as she came it turned around to look at us, swivelled its little head, blinked its big bright eyes and gave a cute little hoot before flying away.

Oh yeah, just while I’m thinking of it: I think that big black cat — something hit me when I read that back — I called it a seed. It made me think of the Datura seeds. It might have been her. I had those two spiky pods hanging upside down in the woodshed. I brought one inside because it had opened and the seeds looked slightly mouldy. They were lying on my desk here, and when we got back from our holiday in January I saw that a mouse had been eating them. I think they like to get high. They ate all my tobacco seeds one year too, little addicts.

It might be connected to her then, the big black cat. I mentioned the mummified cat we found in the barn to Chou the other day and she said it was a dream she had that made us bury her. I’d forgotten that. For a long time we knew there was a mummified cat up there but we did nothing about it. Then Chou had a dream where she was at a sort of taxidermy museum near the coast some place, and somehow the dead animals managed to communicate to her that they weren’t happy, they’d been wronged somehow and wanted her to bury them. So it was after that I buried Mummy Cat.

I don’t plan to ingest the Datura or anything, at most I would perhaps taste a little smoke. I’ve done this once before and it was enough to give me a quick vision. There was this cool gothic witch girl, like out of that movie The Craft, and she was — we were around a fire or something and she was talking to me, trying to tempt me away from my girlfriend somehow. Not ineffectively, I could feel the pull of it. She was sexy, I was tempted. Cat wasn’t very happy when I told her about that. I was being a bit silly or flippant about it I suppose, I don’t know. It’s quite serious stuff though, and she was better at seeing that, I think, than me.

I have a different relationship to darkness now, to black stuff. I don’t completely understand it and it does sometimes terrify me but I don’t think it would tempt me anymore. I think we must have some kind of an arrangement on some level. It is definitely connected to learning, knowledge. I don’t know. No definitely. Apart from that it feels much different from how it used to. There is more of an acknowledgement than an attraction, I could say.

There was one plant growing next to the house here — they both came up late, of their own accord, and only managed to complete a cycle because of the warm autumn we had — and a bigger more mature one behind the shit heap. Also at the shit heap we had a random poisonous pumpkin arrive. Don’t know where it came from but gave a variety of tiny decorative inedible pumpkins. Very pretty and very witchy.

Good gourde.

12:44

When I popped out earlier I spotted the ceramic cooking dish I’d roasted some pork in. Picked up a joint that had gone off the day before at the farm shop on Saturday.

Chou had said, You better put that outside see if any rats want to lick it.

I laughed and did just that.

I wonder if that wasn’t what Old Foxy was on about this morning? The middle of the dish has been licked out. That would be nice if it were they who found the offering!

17:28

Still wasting a bit of time and energy in areas that I’m not quite ready to just jack in or leave behind.

Computer music, sometimes just hack away at a shite thing far too long.

Also I don’t feel massively inspired by work flow or manner of working of most DAWs — batwag has some fun and slick stuff but…

I mean, this is one of the ways I’m wasting.

Just watch it, is the answer, do it.

Every fuck up or whatever is an opportunity to not blah blah blah.

There is much in this log I want to leave well behind.

I want nothing to do with either side of any so-called culture wars, nor do I want to purvey some half-baked philosophy.

I don’t want to do much, let alone too much. Just breathe, garden, work here, and concentrate the best into things worth putting out, worth coming back to, returning to, not for human consumption.

You’ll remember it all right? You don’t need to make some sort of list of mistakes-not-to-make? You are probably right that that would be one of them.

It wasn’t that though. It was:

  1. keep chipping away at Pure Data, being able to do things your way is a worthwhile goal, just don’t get lost in it again.
  2. do Sunday Underwaters, sure, but just play to the dawn chorus — no beats, just keys and use your voice if you want but probably not to read, just to texture, no bpm or any of that.
  3. do word salady fun weird word stuff, either pure voice — defo try that — or with beats, around 90 bpm.
  4. try some short little songs andor decorated poems.
  5. you have ideas you should focus on and implement, like you were thinking earlier of trying to swing from one rhythm to another using a melody to weight on one part of it, anchor, while the other bit changed — your Pure Data system should be experimental fun, but you should try implementing experimental ideas in this manner, not just fiddling about ‘discovering’ stuff.
  6. you really feel it is not to your benefit — or anyone else’s — that you get involved in whatever the world currently imagines its or someone else’s problem is… it doesn’t work somehow. so just to work from what you love, and rage on, into the… kind of stuff that doesn’t jar. that fits with frogs and birdsong and art and treeality. i dunno.
  7. you’ll be fine, you’re just saying get on with it, and there is plenty worth getting on with for all the rough patches or bits that might not work.
