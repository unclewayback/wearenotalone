She just opened the door, and what should come in but a whole lovely lungful of Toddy song, blackbird magic, ha-hey!

I heard him last night actually, but thought it was the thrush at first. Stood there trying to listen for the Thrushian word for snail. Was it wurldut-cheet? I figured deep-boo probably meant ‘to eat’. The sound of him laughing was clear enough, and thus the general tone of their humour: they slide their jokes into the flow of things, rather than having them be an interruption of some sort.

The more I listened the more I began to despair of ever figuring out even a phrase of it. There was so much going on. I got a kind of vertigo at one point, the world began to swim slightly and — that’s it — I sensed the spirit of the fool. The black fool, really. A fool we would fear, the way most fear madness. Heck, let’s just say we all fear madness. You meet it like this, you can’t not.

It begins like a kind of jokey gamboling, a kind of cartwheeled cavortation along the tarzan strings of things. The bright jungle of this Ungléshan Treesigh Jahden, where what you thought was daisies’ll soon be ready to poke their tongues out, and the sound of the bee doesn’t doplar shift past your ear but becomes a How did I get in here? bouncing around the cartooned innards of your suddenly stinging numskull. All the little people inside ye in an uproar of de-description, the green and purple ocean of the world whipping up a storm. All these colours just so much spilt oil upon the void of the insane truth. It’s coming for you. The Black Fool is dancing through the conscious undergrowth, and whose face will it be wearing but the grimacing distortion of your own?

Somehow listening too closely to the birdies can become a mutiny, you know? Who makes the most sense and how? If you’re anything like me you just swim on through, taking mental notes that could come in handy should you reach the other side. Which you can never quite be sure that you have, because maybe that weirdness was it? Maybe you were meant to dive instead of swim? Or is that what got you into this mess in the first place?

Come up for air. The bird seems awful close. You walked past the lilac tree without looking at it, not wanting to scare him away. Now you’re a way aways you can turn and check it out. There he is, right at the top, singing away. No wonder you were a little overwhelmed by the vocabulary. No wonder you couldn’t pick out the Thrushian word for snail. ’tain’t a thrush mush c’est le merle! Tis a blackbird you fool! Yoo woz under thee shpell ov is poemz is awl!

Imagine that: the dawn chorus not some semi-collective territorial tussle, nor a beastly expression of thoughtless relief at a friendlier turn in the temperature of light, but a wild multifarious outburst of What am I?s! These very being’s singings the unanswerable transquestions of themselves.

You don’t even have to imagine it, right?

It is already imagining you.

Anyone who thinks they can tell the difference between consciousness and imagination isn’t alive, I blurted out, maybe more than once upon a time.

A wizard who overheard me had to wonder if I understood consciousness as well as I thought I did, if I would say a thing like that. And anyway, how did I know I wasn’t dead?

Sometimes I’m just trying to fuck with Artificial Intelligences, I said. In any case I think I probably am dead, I’m here aren’t I? As to consciousness and imagination, I guess it depends on how you want to set up your scheme, what you think about fantasy. Differentiating consciousness from imagination is easy enough if you just make consciousness intentional, say it is always consciousness of something. Then consciousness is simply that which is aware it is imagining.

But who’s to say that isn’t the imagination itself?

I believe… that in another dimension… a wizard once made — no, let’s say a craftsman…

(It was west, east, south, north I cut the tree.)

I believe that in another dimension a magical craftsman once found a hawthorn split in two by lightning — actually it was split in four, but the secondary pair of trunks he kept in a secret cupboard in case of emergencies (I once found this cupboard and managed to wrench it open, it was empty, I realise now that he must have hidden the wood in the form of the shelves themselves!) — he made from this tree a pair of magical wands, each the mirror of the other, with the burnt zag of the stripe of the strike the only place where they would perfectly fit together.

These wands were to be gifted to the realm’s soon-to-be-born magical twins. A boy and a girl whose coming had been prophesized by the queen one fabulous night, when a barn owl flew in to the great hall and rolled back her eyes in her head. They were born to her sister eleven months later, just as her strange poem said.

Now the tree has been seen
 opening its never closing eyes
 before the night sky ever sparkled

And the single feather of the doubled spine
 has flown by falling into the text of three fathoms
 to unmask meaning in the forest of unrecorded dreams

So shall the reason-guarded twins be born to the black witch
 whose head spins with the unrecited sayings of another life’s love
 and stirs the whirlpool in her cauldron with a spoon of tomorrow’s light

Under this sign shall come the great disorganisation to lift the horse’s head
 from deep within the unwoven belly of the backwards maid’s wild flowerbed
 and bring beginnings’ thunder to cross over at the broken rainbow’s end:

That bridge which burns with bliss on the ship of we unmortal friends!

(Or, that’s what some learnéd people later said that it had to have said.)

Two wands their first birthday gift from that strange uncle: the craftsman whose very gaze was to amaze.

Two wands whose knobbles they gummed until their teeth came, then bit out their growing pains upon.

Two wands that grew as they did, becoming two great staffs that carried them far, together and apart, and made the holes in the ground they planted their lost teeth into, the seeds that became the skeleton keys.

Two staffs that grew into two trees at the heart of one great city, a city that seeded time with its passageways and laced space with its silkroads of meaning.

Two trees that grew between heaven and earth, whose fallen leaves were sown into the unscripted book that stayed open by virtue of its pages’ implacable weight, and was read behind closed eyes by the light of its own fruit.

A fruit that no being could trade, that effortlessly enforced every boundary through the obliterate geometry of the endlessly changing patterns that emanated from its impossible core.

A fruit that made a wine that, were any absurd enough to try, would infallibly demonstrate the necessity of Cosmic Anarchy, for its very bouquet was to relay the ever returning message that can have gone but One Way.

A wine that would one day give rise to a music that only the silent might hear, and so only the lonely might share...

Which hand? she said, grinning at me with them both behind her back.

The one with the slimy thing in it, I said without skipping a beat.

How did you know? she said, showing me the beautiful newt who had been snuggling in her left palm, orange belly with white freckles all over the lower half of his dark flesh, eyes just another pair of freckles really. I looked into them:

Hello spot, I said, nice to see you.

It’s true, it’s always nice to see a newt. I guess the world outside really is beginning to wake back up.

I’m not massively happy with the little bits I wrote this morning. It was fun at the time. I liked doing her poem. I liked some of the other ideas.

“But you can piss that stuff out your arse. And it’s not Asylum is it? Aren’t you meant to be writing that? And anyway I never would have babies, you fucktard,” spat the black witch inside me, lovingly.

“I wasn’t thinking they would be normal twins, and definitely not that you would have stumbled upon them in any ordinary fashion, nothing that disgusting, but yeah I know what you mean,” I muttered.

“You know the future is no more a child than it is a wrinkly old hag,” she winked.

“Yeah but even then the old hag is still a child, I can see it in your eyes. It’s not really about the future anyway. Or now or the past.”

“Yes yes it’s imagination isn’t it, but what’s that?”

A whole stream of transformations streamed through my head. The tree became book and the book became portal and the portal became city and the city became ship. It’s all Asylum though, somehow, Asylum and Thee Wylds.

“Whatever it is it’s a little bit crazy. And also much more exacting than I thought, considering it could be anything, in a way…” I sighed.

“That way ain’t no way at all, boney pie, at least you got that right.”
