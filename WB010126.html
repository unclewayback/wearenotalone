Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010126

 

January 2nd, 2023

Moanday, 07:10

It’s that blue monday day back at work woe is me day, isn’t it? I should probably find something I really don’t want to do to do.

I had nice musical dreams, so much as I remember. The phrase that sticks in my head from the last one is “Ashock rising from the Urdu drum,” but then I thought his name changed to A from an N one, was it Nerdu? Or Nertius? The latter sounds too Roman. Pretty sure it wasn’t Ashoka. Will have to check. Energetic blue fella with a pointy little electric moustache and a touch of gargoyle, or Angry God, about him. A Djinn or a Genie, a touch of that, too. He was in the drum like a genie in a lamp, I guess, and the artwork was of the drum being played, and it was like him being smacked on the head, and rising up out of it all along the log was a kind of story, of his release, I thought, but perhaps it was his release and entrapment intertwined.

It was a friend of mine — well, no, someone I didn’t know before who I was making, or hoping to make, friends with through this activity. He had a substance that sounded like ‘tartar’, which was a crumbly brown-grey dust, and he used this to sprinkle inside cut tree stumps, to hollow them out and make drums. But it also seemed to burn a design into them.

With my long log he was reticent, telling me it was so long it would probably exhaust his supply, but I encouraged him a little bit and I think in fact he was quite up for it. Maybe he was curious as to what would happen, as I don’t think he’d done a long quite thin log before. It sparks up like an indoor firework, and in smoke and sparkles and a smooth and stylishly filmed rotation the design appears — already in full colour.
