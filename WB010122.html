Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010122

 

January 6th, 2023

16:51 The English Channel

I guess, unless otherwise mentioned, it is WB ain tit? Home at Winding Brook. Also quite at home on the swaying sea, it seems. It is not nothing, this little ocean of ours.

I know, I know it is not an ocean. It was the swaying sea that did it to me. Swayed me towards inaccuracy. I wonder if there is something inherently alliterative about it? That ruffled expanse, that great rolling surface.

It gets a little rocky out here, in the extensive centre.

The Sea King doesn’t like it when boats like ours are rude enough to fly over his Mansions. His wife is in the garden, waving her arms at the sky. That’s the waves.

We have found ourselves quite a handsome lounge. A nice little nook with facing seats. I drifted off to sleep reading about Seely Wights. I danced with Dolphins, sure.

She came and reclined—

Look a ganet!

—in the seat next to mine. After I woke up. Rested across the awkward leather arm rest. One thread of the blue wool of her jumper seems to be woven through the greys of mine. She wears jeans, a light, light blue scarf. I have dark blue tweed trousers. My eyes are blue, hers grey blue. The sky and the sea. We have dressed appropriately.

I think of telling her we are no ordinary family, trying to ease her anxieties in the car on the way over. She wouldn’t spend a week with anyone. I tell her we are mad. We only judge the judgers.

It echoes through my mind: we are no ordinary family. I think of us all on our journeys. All those Safe Journeys. I wonder if we might not be Gods?

I feel a steel made of these elements. A sword that sings with all the swaying of the sea. That has a soul, that is never still — whose very stillness, solidity… is in a thing that is not there. Not here. All around us now, everywhere. Not there.

A metal made from the light that falls upon the waves. That is my blood, my soul, the colour of my eyes, the wearing of my clothes, my way.

We will have to move now. Not such a handsome lounge after all. Not when filled with the untertaining nonsense of the cinema next door. She has already moved. I wanted to finish a paragraph or two.

My angel
 is an invisible
 disability

You, my family,
 we are Gods.

I turned from the Earth
 to marry the woman of the Oceans
 who makes her sea sick

All together now
 we make for The Mountains

To make more than memories

Eye contact

(After talking about falling asleep reading, her saying how making the whole journey tonight would not have been possible, me remembering taking Ryan’s Mum’s car to her in Ireland, driving up to Holyhead, a rocky drunken sleepless crossing, going through the fields in the early morning, nodding off and waking up on my way to the ditch. Drunk driving, early one morning after an all nighter in my early twenties, with Cat, pulling up beside Winton Rec, knowing I’d done well to get even that far, unable to hold the car straight, throwing the keys as far as I could across the frosty playing field, the laughing search of the need to sober up, some part of me only just wise enough to let the rest of me be lucky. Another drunken moment with Ryan, giving some girl the I’m-a-Philologist shpiel, then taking her through an elaborate history of the word Cunt, via the obligatory witchy sexiness of an Ancient Egyptian Cat Goddess. I think he was genuinely proud of me, if only for that one night.)

(I’ll probably stay here, hold fort. The cinema sounds are intermittent and these seats are too pleasant. I defended them from a haughty disabled woman who I quickly judged as pushing around the one who was pushing her around. He was taking her on through but she thought she might squeeze in in our direction. I leant across to Chou and told her we would move if we had to, but they just went on past. That’s how I justified my evil disinclination to give up this wide prize place: that my wife’s disability was simply invisible. And so is Our Angel. And so is the metal that runs through my blood. That sings with a sea between lands. A sky between night and day.)

(James Brown now. I can tolerate about four seconds of funky cheese before I want to push it down the stairs. The original meaning ov bwoken beatz.)

(Nearly finished my Murphys. 17:38. Another couple of hours then.)
