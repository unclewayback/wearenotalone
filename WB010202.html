My migraines are back, you see. I had the second one last night, and I think it was two nights before that — the gap of a day prior — it first returned. First migraine since I feel I managed to draw them out while reading Jung’s Red Book, whenever that was, some years ago now.

Same migraines, although the headaches are not nearly as bad as I remember. They start from a region that is associated with the lower middle of my left eye. First time it was very much a square, last night a little more undecidable. The bottom of it is pulsatory and indefinite, waves go in and out of it, the top is very sharp and bright. It was very colourful last night, quite blue and red. There is always plenty white, and I can see all the colours of the spectrum in it. It interferes with my vision, being stronger than I what I see ‘out there’ with open eyes.

This sharp top line, vibrating bright and jagged, is the core of it. It tends towards my left. I notice it, and when I do so it is smaller and lower and a little dimmer than it will be. But it is bright enough to notice, I know that this is the beginning of an ‘attack’. It becomes more intense, and it also grows in size and begins to move up my visual field, up and off to the left, where it will eventually disappear. Before it disappears it will spend a few minutes covering nearly my entire visual field, making seeing rather difficult. The whole episode lasts perhaps half an hour. I cannot do anything while it is happening. Once it is over I am left with a vague headache. I am sure that when they used to happen before, there was nothing vague about the headache at all.

I don’t know if it merely highlights a certain disorder, or simply overactivity, in my mind, or is actually associated with it. I think they are simply currently coinciding, however I do associate the migraines with periods of intense thought, and also computer work. I may have to do less of this.

I think of fractals sometimes. They sometimes come up around here. But just now I can’t think why. I only recall that we consider them quantitively infinite. You can zoom in or out forever, and nothing really changes, because the essence of the equation, or simply the mathematical structure, is based on the self-similar. When you have a mathematical structure that all the forms have in common, are generated by, you have self-similarity. When people bemoan abstraction, generalisation, theory, and celebrate the concrete, the specific, practise — it is effectively this, self-similar, form of repetition, or changelessness, that they are ‘demonising’.
