Titles Should Come At The End

A house full of muffled meanings
 to bother the peripheries of your non
 sense making: the awkward-bottomed passenger
 who rides the day car’s to do list
 into the instant sunset of that one
 fly-squashed thought, the afterthought of whom
 is always you should have been kinder to.

A head fool of insignificant things
 making purposeful mistakes in the game
 we call life is not a game, unless it is
 all the same to you, Strange Peacock in the long gone
 life tree of the unreturning unrelenting spring,
 constant wood fountain of the drum
 whose every slow slow beat overlapping is the hum
 of hardly heard imagination. Or is it? I can’t quite be sure.

A handy body laid open to grasping erosion as if
 somewhere between the antiriver and ungravity
 a single-feathered beyond could snuggle its
 straightfaced smuggled goods
 into the Big Bad Wolf World of granular analysis —
 mooded atoms of schizophrenic greatness
 bow-tied with light and shuffling into the hanging hall
 where at last the unnumbered shall be weighed
 and counted altogether golden one stubbornly museful lump.

An unrealized ending tethered frayed
 to the brave fiery face in the mirror of the waters
 wide mouth hot with intemperate sayings
 about of and or else any other operative it can’t
 get its rough-lucked tongue round, its not-saying
 nay-roar, burbling rush of languaged liquid
 like
 the beak-gift of a phoenix to the chick of its chosen world:
 My Happy Sickness Names You Father
 (and blurts out the whites of the eyes)

intriguing mention of Equilibrium in Ehrlich’s Hunting The Faster Than Light Tachyon, end page 55 start page 56:

According to “Niven’s Law” of time travel:
“If physical laws governing our universe permit the possibility of backwards-time signaling or time travel which can change the past, then no devices for doing either one will be invented.”
In other words, a message sent to the past alters the entire future history, including the sending of the original message and its content. The altered message will change the past in a different way, and so on, until some “equilibrium” is reached — the simplest being where no message at all is sent. Backward time signaling thus erases itself.
