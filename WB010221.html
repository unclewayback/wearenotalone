Sam Knot | Winding Book | 010221

 

22nd February

07:30

The first thing you should know is that Mushi the Bunny wasn’t always his name. His previous companion had called him Bishi, meaning Bogey in Chinese. The Knots didn’t feel right calling him that but they wanted a word which sounded similar just in case he had gotten used to it. So they called him Mushishi, or Mushi for short, although this would turn out to be just one of his many names.

Mushi is a Japanese word meaning Insect, Mushishi was the title of the main character in an anime the Knots were fond of, featuring an itinerant shaman who wandered the Japanese countryside solving problems caused by the people’s general ignorance regarding the invisible — at least to some — creatures they lived their lives among. These little nature spirits were the Mushi Mushishi was named for. A doctor of visionary insects.

Mister Knot also found himself spontaneously calling him Monk. This may have had something to do with the way he tucked himself into a neat little ball, folding his front paws underneath him as if he were meditating and sitting like that for long periods without moving, for all the world like an ocean-smoothed stone. His fur was white and grey, mostly white with little swathes and ripples of grey, his head half and half. Madame Knot called him Little Cloud and this became his poetic name.

One day Mister Knot was talking to a Wizard on the internet and he mentioned Mushishi, thinking perhaps that because magicians liked pulling rabbits out of hats they would be of interest to Wizards as well. The Wizard was curious, for it wasn’t so long ago he had encountered a random bunny rabbit hopping along the streets of London, no doubt on the tail end of some adventure or other and on his way back to his little girl’s bedroom.

Mister Knot told the Wizard that the rabbit’s poetic name was Little Cloud and the Wizard said it sounded quite Chinese and showed him how it would be written if it were — the Wizard was something of a sinologist, you see — he said the name reminded him of another Cloud: Xu Yun, Empty Cloud, a famous Zen Master of the modern period. Mister Knot was very pleased by this as whenever a new animal arrived in his life he liked to play the game of asking the question of what it might mean.

The Clouds seemed a sizable avenue.
